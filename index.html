Enjoy The world cup season in Qatar

The 22nd FIFA World Cup is being hosted in Qatar from 20 November to 18 December 2022; it is the second World Cup to be held fully in Asia after the 2002 event in South Korea and Japan. It is the first World Cup to be staged in the Arab and Muslim worlds. The 2018 final saw France defeat Croatia 4-2 to retain their title. The cost of the World Cup is estimated to be over $220 billion, making it the most expensive World Cup ever. Qatari officials, including the organising CEO Nasser Al Khater, dispute this estimate and claim that the real cost is $8 billion. Other figures pertain to the overall infrastructure development since Qatar was given the World Cup in 2010.

Group Stages

Competing countries were divided into eight groups of four teams (groups A to H). Teams in each group played one another in a round-robin, where the top two teams advanced to the knockout stage.

Knockout Stages

In the knockout stage, if the scores are equal when normal playing time expires, extra time will be played for two periods of 15 minutes each. This will be followed, if required, by a penalty shoot-out to determine the winners

The finale

The 2022 FIFA World Cup Final will be the final match of the 2022 World Cup, the 22nd edition of FIFA's competition for men's national football teams. The match is scheduled to be played at the Lusail Iconic Stadium in Lusail, Qatar, on 18 December 2022, the national day of Qatar, and will be contested by the winners of the semi-finals.
